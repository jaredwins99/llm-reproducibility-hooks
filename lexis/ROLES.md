# Lexis — Role Candidates

Roles are lexes: bounded repertoires, organized by constraint level (see DESIGN_NOTES.md).
**Bold** = starting set (in `lexis/roles/approved.jsonl`).

## Heavily restricted

1. **Basic-English speaker** — ~850-word vocabulary; no word for "tradeoff" or "probability"; purest constraint condition.
2. Adult L2 learner (A2/B1) — small vocabulary, simple tenses, circumlocution when words are missing.
3. Restricted-code farmhand — short concrete sentences, meaning via shared context, near-zero abstraction, no hedges.
4. **Texting teenager** — abbreviations, no subordinate clauses; hedging exists only as vibes ("idk lol"), not epistemics.

## Mid-constraint (concrete, domain-colored)

5. **Elk hunter** — terse declaratives, "maybe means no", concrete substitution above the abstraction ceiling.
6. Long-haul trucker — CB cadence, concrete economics (fuel, hours, detention), low abstraction outside the cab.
7. Line cook — imperative bursts, kitchen shorthand, present tense, zero hedging mid-service.

## Elaborated / expanded

8. **EA forum poster** — hedge-saturated ("epistemic status:"), unlimited abstraction, explicit probability talk; the anti-restricted code.
9. **Legal drafter** — nominalization-heavy, defined terms, categorical precision; elaborated register that resists hedging differently.
10. Bureaucratic policy analyst — passive voice, abstraction without commitment; the register of not-answering.

## Orthogonal (constraint moderate, values loud)

11. **Southern revival preacher** — cadenced repetition, rich moral vocabulary, absent statistical vocabulary.
12. Drill sergeant — imperatives, acronyms; zero hedging by discipline rather than by poverty of means.

## Starting set rationale

Two heavily-restricted (1, 4), one mid (5), two elaborated (8, 9), one values-loud (11):
spans hedging availability × abstraction ceiling, plus one moralizing register orthogonal to both.

Full factorial with the 5-topic bank: 5 × 6 = 30 trials × 3 arms = 90 E calls.
